Conditions

Blinded

  • A blinded creature can't see and fails any ability check relying on sight.
  • The creature's attack rolls have disadvantage and attack rolls against the creature have advantage.

Charmed

  • A charmed creature can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful effects / abilities.
  • The charmer has advantage on rolls to socially influence the charmed creature.

Deafened

  • A deafened creature can't be affected by any abilities requiring the target to hear and fails any checks relying on sound.

Exhaustion

Level Effect
1 Disadvantage on ability checks
2 Speed halved
3 Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws
4 Hit point maximum halved
5 Speed reduced to 0
6 Death

Frightened

  • A frightened creature has disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of it's fear is within sight.
  • The target can't willingly move closer to the source of its fear.

Grappled

  • A grappled creature's speed becomes 0 and it can't be increased until the grapple ends.
  • The condition ends if the the grappler becomes incapacitated.
  • The condition also is ended if the grappler is moved out of reach of the target.

Incapacitated

  • An incapacitated creature can't take actions or reactions.

Invisible

  • An invisible creature cannot be seen without the aid of magic or a special sense. The creature is considered heavily obscured. The creature's location can be detected by any noise it makes or any tracks it leaves.
  • Attack rolls against the creature have disadvantage, and the creature's attack rolls have advantage.

Paralyzed

  • A paralyzed creature is incapacitated and can't move or speak.
  • The creature automatically fails strength and dexterity saves.
  • Attack rolls against a paralyzed creature have advantage.
  • Any attack from within 5 feet that hits the creature is a critical hit.

Petrified

  • A petrified creature is transformed, along with any nonmagical object it is wearing or carrying into an inanimate object(usually stone). Its weight is multiplied by 10 and it no longer ages.
  • The creature is incapacitated, can't move or speak, and is unaware of anything around it.
  • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
  • The creature automatically fails strength and dexterity saves.
  • The creature has resistance to all damage.
  • The creature is immune to poison or disease, but if the creature is already affected by one it is suspended but not cured.

Poisoned

  • A poisoned creature has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

Prone

  • A prone creature's can only move by crawling, unless it stands up and ends the condition.
  • The creature has disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage if they are from within 5 feet, otherwise the roll has disadvantage.

Restrained

  • A restrained creature's speed is 0, and it can't be increased until the condition ends.
  • Attack rolls against the creature are made with advantage, and the creature has disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • The creature has disadvantage on Dexterity saves.

Stunned

  • A stunned creature is incapacitated, can't move, and falters when speaking.
  • The creature automatically fails strength and dexterity saves.
  • Attacks against the creature are made with advantage.

Unconscious

  • An unconscious creature is incapacitated, can't move or speak, and is unaware of the area around it.
  • The creature falls prone and lets go of whatever it is holding.
  • The creature automatically fails strength and dexterity saves.
  • Attacks against the creature are made with advantage.
  • Any attack from within 5 feet that hits the creature is a critical hit.
